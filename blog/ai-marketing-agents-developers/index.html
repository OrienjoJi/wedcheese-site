How Deterministic Gates Keep Content On-Brand

AI-generated content carries real risks: factual errors, brand misalignment, compliance violations, or tone drift. The platform uses a deterministic safety spine—validators, a guardian layer, and fail-closed gates—to ensure every piece of content meets your standards before publication.

Here's how it works: agents propose content based on your product facts and approved claims. Code-based validators check every output against your rules. A guardian layer enforces guardrails. If any check fails, the gates close and content is rejected. There is no bypass. The LLM proposes, code decides.

This approach eliminates the guesswork of prompt engineering and the risk of unexpected outputs. Your marketing team gets deterministic behavior, not probabilistic luck.

Running Marketing at Developer Velocity

The platform is built for teams shipping fast. Multi-tenant architecture with row-level-security isolation means you can spin up new products or brands without friction. Each agent inherits your documented product facts and approved claims, so consistency scales as you do.

You define the guardrails once. Deploy agents across multiple products. Watch content flow without human bottlenecks. The result: a marketing team that moves at code velocity, not hiring velocity.

Your job stays focused on building. The agents handle the rest.
