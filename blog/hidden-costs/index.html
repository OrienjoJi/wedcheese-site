The 5 Hidden Wedding Costs That Will Ruin Your Budget

Published June 2026~5 min read

"Our eyes are bigger than our bank account." It's the most common realisation couples have about two months into wedding planning.

You might have a rough number in your head for the venue and the dress, but it's the little, unbudgeted expenses that slowly drain your funds. To keep your finances in check, you need a dedicated budget planner for your wedding.

The most forgotten wedding expenses

Before you sign any contracts, make sure you have accounted for these five sneaky budget-breakers:

  1. Vendor meals: Your photographer, DJ, and coordinator are working 8–10 hour shifts. You are contractually obligated to feed them, which means adding them to your catering headcount!
  2. Postage for invitations: Heavy cardstock or unusually shaped envelopes cost significantly more to mail than standard letters.
  3. Taxes and gratuities: A 20% service charge and local taxes can add thousands to a catering bill that initially looked affordable.
  4. Dress alterations: The price tag on the dress is rarely the final cost. Hemming, taking it in, or adding a bustle can cost hundreds of pounds.
  5. Overtime fees: If the reception runs late and the DJ keeps playing, expect to pay premium hourly overtime rates.
