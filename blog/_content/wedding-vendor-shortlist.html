Vendor research starts as excitement—Pinterest boards, venue open days, a photographer whose work makes you cry. Within weeks it becomes tabs, screenshots, half-sent emails, and three florists you cannot tell apart. A proper wedding vendor shortlist turns that noise into a pipeline you can actually book from.

The couples who book calmly are rarely the ones who contacted forty photographers. They are the ones who defined categories, compared like for like, and moved on politely when a supplier went quiet. This guide walks through that process—and where AI vendor search saves time without making decisions for you.

Start with categories, not names

Before you fall in love with a single supplier, list what you need for your day. Venue and catering usually come first because they lock date and capacity. Photography, music, florals, hair and make-up, and transport follow in a sensible order for your timeline. Our wedding vendor checklist shows which suppliers to book and when.

Give each category a budget ceiling from your category split so research stays grounded. Chasing a photographer who costs half your total budget is only painful if you discover it late.

Research with your real wedding context

A florist perfect for a barn may look flat in a city hotel. Search with location, guest count, season, and style in mind—not generic “best wedding photographer UK” lists. WedCheese’s AI vendor search uses your wedding profile to surface suppliers near your venue and suggest what to compare before you enquire.

Save promising options immediately with notes: travel fees, minimum spend, peak-season premiums, and vibe tags (documentary, editorial, relaxed). That detail matters when you return weeks later and cannot remember why you shortlisted someone.

Build a shortlist of three per category

Three is enough to compare without drowning in quotes. For each candidate, capture website, contact, rating, quote (when you have it), and status: saved, contacted, quoted, or hired. When a supplier goes quiet, you move to the next name without starting research from scratch.

Before you email anyone, read our guide to questions to ask a wedding venue and adapt similar sharp questions for photographers, caterers, and celebrants. Specific enquiries get faster replies than vague “are you available?” messages.

Compare quotes fairly

Quotes only help when they describe the same scope: hours on site, staff ratios, delivery, VAT, overtime, and cancellation terms. Use an apples-to-apples checklist—our vendor quote comparison guide walks through the line items couples miss. Log numbers beside each saved vendor so your budget reflects reality before you sign.

If one quote sits far below the others, ask what is excluded rather than assuming you have found a bargain. Hidden fees belong in your notes, not a surprise invoice eight weeks out.

Move from shortlist to booking

When you are ready to commit, confirm payment milestones in writing and update your deposit tracker the same day. Mark the hired vendor, archive the runners-up politely, and free mental space for the next category.

Send a brief “we went another direction” note to runners-up if they invested time in calls or custom quotes. Goodwill matters in tight markets—you may need a backup supplier later.

Common shortlist mistakes to avoid

  • Booking on vibe alone. Portfolio chemistry matters, but contracts, insurance, and backup plans matter more.
  • Comparing quotes with different guest counts. Catering and bar packages shift dramatically at 80 versus 110 covers.
  • Letting inbox silence stall the whole category. Set a follow-up date, then advance to your next shortlisted name.
  • Forgetting travel and setup fees. A “local” band may still charge for early load-in or late breakdown.

If negotiation makes you nervous, read how to negotiate with vendors before you sign—collaborative scripts beat last-minute haggling.

Season and location filters

Peak Saturdays in popular postcodes book fast—your shortlist should include at least one supplier available on your actual date, not only your dream date. Ask about associate photographers, second teams, or mid-week discounts early. Rural venues may need vendors willing to travel; confirm mileage before you emotionally commit on Instagram.

AI search helps here by weighting proximity and category together, so you are not manually mapping every enquiry. Still verify insurance, backup equipment, and cancellation terms yourself—search results are a starting point, not due diligence.

Revisit your shortlist monthly during peak booking season; availability changes fast for Saturdays in summer.
