A good wedding planning app should help when the work becomes messy: changing guests, chasing vendors, adjusting a budget, and turning ideas into next steps. WedCheese gives couples an AI wedding planner app that understands context around budget, guests, vendors, decor, checklist, and timeline. The question is when paying for Premium actually earns its keep.

Free is enough to test the workflow

Free users can start planning and ask a limited number of AI questions each day—enough to build your first checklist, log a budget, and see how the assistant responds to your wedding profile. Use that phase to decide whether connected planning beats scattered notes. Compare with generic tools in our AI planner vs ChatGPT guide.

Premium is for active planning weeks

Premium increases daily AI capacity from a handful of questions to many more—useful when you are comparing vendors, refining categories, drafting emails, and running scenarios in one evening. It is not a magic planner; it is headroom so you are not rationing help during crunch time.

What to ask when you have Premium room

  • Realistic budget splits after guest count changes—see guest count and budget.
  • Vendor email drafts before contacting photographers, venues, or florists—pair with awkward emails templates.
  • Decor ideas that match venue, season, and palette.
  • Next-task clarity when your timeline checklist feels overwhelming.

When Premium is probably not worth it

If you are barely opening the app, still months from decisions, or only need occasional wording help, free may suffice. Premium pays back when planning has momentum—supplier comparisons, final headcount, seating, and vendor confirmations landing in the same fortnight.

Start free, upgrade when sessions get interrupted by daily limits, and downgrade if life quiets down after the wedding week. The best upgrade moment is when AI admin saves you another hour of inbox dread—not when you hope software will make emotional choices for you.

How Premium fits the WedCheese workflow

WedCheese Premium is not a separate product—it is more daily room inside the same app you already use for checklist, budget, guests, vendors, and decor. That matters because Premium only pays off when you are living in the workspace, not visiting once a fortnight for a speech joke.

Busy weeks often cluster: after save-the-dates, after invitations, and four to six weeks before the wedding when seating, dietary exports, and vendor confirmations collide. If those windows align with your engagement, Premium is likely worth a month or two—not necessarily the entire twelve-month plan.

Free features you still keep

Guest lists, RSVP links, budget tracking, vendor saving, timeline tasks, and decor planning remain available on free. Premium changes how often you can ask the AI for help drafting emails, comparing scenarios, or untangling next steps—not whether you can plan at all. Review what couples need from AI planner apps before you upgrade on features you may already have.

Try before the crunch

Use free AI capacity during early planning to learn how you like to prompt—vendor emails, decor ideas, task breakdowns. When invitations go out and replies accelerate, you will already know which questions to ask instead of learning the tool under stress. That habit makes Premium feel like extra runway, not a rescue purchase.

Cancel when the wedding week ends

Subscriptions you forgot to review become the hidden cost of planning. Set a calendar reminder a week after the wedding to assess whether you still need Premium—most couples do not. The memories matter; the daily AI limit does not.
