Research before you bargain

Shortlist by category and capture baseline pricing before emotional tastings cloud judgement. Note peak-season premiums, minimum hours, travel zones, and assistant counts. Track inquiry → proposal → booked stages so follow-ups stay polite. Our quote comparison guide lists the lines couples miss.

Quote literacy checklist

  • Scope parity: guest counts, hours on site, delivery, staffing ratios.
  • Fees: service charges, VAT, overtime, parking, cleanup.
  • Contingency: weather clauses for tents, outdoor sound, photo locations.
  • Cancellation: deposits retained versus transferable credits.
  • Milestones: payment percentages aligned with cash flow.

Scripts that sound collaborative

Lead with gratitude, then isolate the gap: “We love your direction. Comparing like for like, Package B at another supplier includes a second shooter until speeches end. Is there room to mirror that at our guest count, or should we trim hours?” Swap in comparisons from saved quotes. If budgets are firm, propose value swaps—smaller florals for extended coverage, weekday rehearsal instead of add-ons—rather than blunt discount asks.

Negotiate once from shared facts

Vendors remember couples who read contracts and pay on time more than couples who squeeze every penny without clarity. Update your budget categories immediately after you sign. Negotiate from facts; let your tracker reflect the outcome the same day.

Timing matters

Off-peak Saturdays, weekday weddings, and shoulder-season dates give you leverage suppliers understand. Ask about package trims in January when summer calendars are filling—not six weeks before when teams are booked solid. Early booking sometimes earns modest incentives; late bargaining rarely does.

Know what is usually non-negotiable

Insurance, certified staff, minimum call-out hours, and rush fees protect the vendor’s business—and your event. Focus energy on scope and timing swaps instead of asking professionals to match hobbyist prices. If a quote still fails your allocation after fair comparison, thank them and move to the next name on your shortlist without ghosting.

Document what you agreed

Email a bullet summary after verbal concessions: hours, deliverables, payment dates, and excluded items. Vendors appreciate clarity; you appreciate evidence if memory drifts before the contract arrives. Link the final figure to your deposit tracker the moment you sign.

Bring social proof carefully

Mentioning competing quotes is fair when comparisons are genuine—never invent a lower price to pressure a supplier. Photographers and florists talk; reputations linger longer than one discount. Negotiate as the couple you want vendors to recommend to future clients.
