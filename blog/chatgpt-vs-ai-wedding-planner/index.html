ChatGPT vs. A Dedicated AI Wedding Planner: Which is Better?

Published June 2026~9 min read

"Can I just use ChatGPT to plan my wedding?" It is the most common question we hear from couples who already pay for a general AI subscription. The honest answer: ChatGPT is excellent at writing—speeches, polite emails, brainstorming themes—but it is not an ai wedding planner in the sense that matters when you are juggling deposits, RSVPs, and a date that will not move.

Generic AI forgets your context between chats. It cannot see that you are £800 over on florals, that twelve guests have not replied, or that your ceremony starts at 2pm and your photographer only booked until 10pm. A dedicated planner closes that gap.

What ChatGPT does well for weddings

  • Drafting maid-of-honour speech outlines and thank-you note wording.
  • Brainstorming themes, colour palettes, and hashtag ideas.
  • Explaining etiquette questions in plain language.
  • Rewriting awkward emails to vendors or family (with you pasting in the details).

Treat it like a clever writing assistant—not your project manager.

Where generic AI falls short

  • No live wedding profile. You re-explain budget, date, and guest count every session.
  • No actions. It suggests "book a florist" but cannot search vendors, save quotes, or add a timeline task.
  • No schedule builder. It can describe a run sheet; it cannot generate one from your actual events and travel times.
  • No safety rails. It might propose a £40k upgrade when you said £15k three messages ago.
  • Copy-paste fatigue. Every update means exporting from your spreadsheet back into the chat.

What a dedicated ai wedding planner should do instead

The bar for a real ai wedding planner is higher than fluent text. It should behave like an agent: read your data, propose changes, and execute approved updates inside the app.

  • Know your current budget, categories, and headcount without being reminded.
  • Generate and adjust checklist tasks based on your wedding date—including accelerated timelines for short engagements.
  • Search vendors near your venue and log results beside your budget.
  • Draft proposals you review before anything is written to your profile.
